Farming Guides

Most people assume farming, without context, means sitting around waiting for plants to grow. In Final Fantasy XIV, this terminology is referring to spending time to gather or obtain specific materials or gear. The majority of the playerbase spends many hours to many months grinding sought after gears and mounts (more in their section) that it can be overwhelming when starting off on what to get and where to get it. In the guides that follow, each section would break down certain aspects of what and where to farm certain items.

If you are not new to the MMO world, you may have heard something called BiS being thrown around the communities. These are highly coveted gear pieces that many players attempt to obtain to make their damage numbers reach the absolute best they can. Thankfully, not every piece of gear is BiS and you can get by with a lot less, especially if you are uncomfortable with how "competative" certain activities can be. The beauty of Final Fantasy XIV is that you can gear yourself however you want and still be successful at it. The only thing is that as long as you hit the minimum iLvl requirement, you will be cleared to participate in certain contents. A lot of the gear pieces you will be earning can actually be repurposed as well, granting you additional resources and currencies. One such place is the Doman Enclave, unlocked after you have completed Stormblood as a psuedo Ishguardian Restoration, which is the hub for early game crafters wanting to speed up their level gains.

What to farm?

Farming is inherently and fundamentally required for any MMORPG. You are not excluded just because you crafted the best gear or got the best items; you are constantly spending time and resources getting better and better. Sometimes you find another class is better for certain features or just want to play something different, getting back to on par gearsets as others have been doing it this entire time. A lot of content could be "classified" as farmable content, but the rewards dictate what you are farming. For example, all of the end game content gives two sets of tomestones, one is uncapped and the other one is weekly capped. You won't spend your time farming the end-game dungeons for gear if the prize of getting your capped tomestones is more beneficial in the long run.
